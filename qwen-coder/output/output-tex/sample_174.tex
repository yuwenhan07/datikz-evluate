Certainly! Below is a TikZ LaTeX code to generate an iterated blowup of a \( K_4 \) graph, where the "thick" edges indicate that all possible edges between the sets of vertices are given the specified color.

```latex
\documentclass{standalone}
\usepackage{tikz}

\begin{document}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=2]

% Define colors
\colorlet{blueedge}{blue}
\colorlet{rededge}{red}

% Draw the original K4
\node (v1) at (-2, 2) [circle, draw] {};
\node (v2) at (0, 2) [circle, draw] {};
\node (v3) at (2, 2) [circle, draw] {};
\node (v4) at (0, 0) [circle, draw] {};

% Connect v1-v2, v2-v3, v3-v1, v1-v4, v2-v4, v3-v4
\draw[thick, blueedge] (v1) -- (v2);
\draw[thick, blueedge] (v2) -- (v3);
\draw[thick, blueedge] (v3) -- (v1);
\draw[thick, rededge] (v1) -- (v4);
\draw[thick, rededge] (v2) -- (v4);
\draw[thick, rededge] (v3) -- (v4);

% Blow up each vertex into two parts
% For v1
\node (v1a) at (-2.5, 2.5) [circle, fill=white, inner sep=1pt]{};
\node (v1b) at (-2.5, 1.5) [circle, fill=white, inner sep=1pt]{};

% For v2
\node (v2a) at (-1.5, 2.5) [circle, fill=white, inner sep=1pt]{};
\node (v2b) at (-1.5, 1.5) [circle, fill=white, inner sep=1pt]{};
