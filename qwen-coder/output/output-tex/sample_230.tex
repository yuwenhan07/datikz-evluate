To create a TikZ diagram that represents a game \( G \) with an honest history \((r_A, l_B)\), we need to understand the components involved:

1. **Players**: Two players, Player A (\(A\)) and Player B (\(B\)).
2. **Actions**: Player A has actions \( r_A \) and \( l_A \). Player B has actions \( r_B \) and \( l_B \).
3. **History**: The history given is \((r_A, l_B)\), which means Player A chose \( r_A \) and Player B chose \( l_B \).

We will represent this in a simple decision tree format where each node represents a player's choice and the edges represent the resulting outcomes.
