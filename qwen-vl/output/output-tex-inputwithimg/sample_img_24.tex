\section*{Illustration of Online NDWT Pyramidal Algorithm for \( J = 3 \), \( W = 2 \)}

Input time series given by top row of coefficients. Coefficients with dotted borders obtained by constant-end extension. Dashed arrows denote filtering operations with \(\mathcal{G}\), solid arrows denote filtering operations with \(\mathcal{H}\).

\begin{figure}[h]
    \centering
    \begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.8, transform shape]
        % Define styles for nodes

            mydottednode/.style={rectangle, draw=black, dashed, fill=white, minimum size=1cm},
            myarrow/.style={-{Latex[length=3mm, width=3mm]}, thick},
            mydottedarrow/.style={-{Latex[length=3mm, width=3mm]}, dashed, thick}
        }
        
        % Nodes for T=1
        \matrix (T1) [matrix of nodes, row sep=1cm, column sep=1cm] {
            \node[mynode] (c2_0) {$c_{2,0}$}; & & \\
            & \node[mynode] (c2_1) {$c_{2,1}$}; & \\
            & & \node[mynode] (d1_1) {$d_{1,1}$}; \\
            & \node[mynode] (c1_1) {$c_{1,1}$}; & \\
            & & \node[mynode] (d0_1) {$d_{0,1}$}; \\
            & & \node[mynode] (v0_1) {$v_{0,1}$}; \\

            & & \node[mynode] (v0_2) {$v_{0,2}$}; \\

        % Dotted border nodes
        \node[mynode, dotted, above left=of c2_0] (c2_0_dotted) {};
