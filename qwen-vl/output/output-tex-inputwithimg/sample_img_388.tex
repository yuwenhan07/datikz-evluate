\textbf{Asymptotic alignment} $\zeta^+ = \max(\zeta, 0)$ between the signal $\vy$ and the dominant eigenvector of $\rmT^{(2)} \rmT^{(2) \top}$, as defined in Theorem \ref{thm:spike2}, with $c_1 = \frac{1}{2}$, $c_2 = \frac{1}{3}$ and $c_3 = \frac{1}{6}$. The curve $\zeta = 0$ is the position of the \textbf{phase transition} between the impossible detectability of the signal (below) and the presence of an isolated eigenvalue in the spectrum of $\rmT^{(2)} \rmT^{(2) \top}$ with corresponding eigenvector correlated with the signal (above). It has an asymptote $\beta_M = (\frac{c_1 c_2}{1 - c_3})^{1 / 4}$, represented by the red dashed line, as $\rho_T \to +\infty$.
